Digital Art Projects

This piece is part of a series of illustrations from “Cathay” by Steven Millhauser. I used crosshatching and color shades to portray the texture of the waves. The zoomed-out view and blue hue are consistent throughout all illustrations in the series.

“Floating Islands”

    2024

    2024

“Shoes”

    2026

  • Digital Illustration

This piece is a work of directing attention. I limited the color palette in this work to three colors, used as a highlight, midtone, and shadow. The challenge of this work was to draw attention away from the figure, therefore bringing the focus to the shoes.

“Obsession”

    2026

  • Digital Illustration / Texture/Linework / Transition from Traditional to Digital

This piece is a work on textures, experimenting with different techniques and linework in order to achieve different contours and textures. This work was also a challenge in transferring traditional artwork and line into digital media.

“Who’s Parked Outside?”

    2026

  • Photoshop / Collage / Exposure / Shadows

This photograph is a collage of three images: a driveway and two toy vehicles. This piece is part of a series that reimagines small objects in a life-size setting. Utilizing adjustments in exposure and drop shadow, I integrated the vehicles into the setting realistically.

“On Her Perch”

    2026

  • Photoshop / Digital Collage / Collage

This photograph is a composite of two images: a building scape and a ceramic dragon. This piece is part of a series reimagining small items in a life-sized setting. I used adjustments in exposure, clarity, and color balance to bring the ceramic to a similar tonal range to the building. Using the selection and brightness adjustments, I dropped the shadow from the ceramic.

“Looking Out at the Water”

    2026

  • Photoshop / Digital Collage / Shadows

This photograph is a composite of two images: the pillar on the shoreline and the figure. I utilized layer masking, color balance, and adjustments in exposure to make the two images merge harmoniously. I also added the dropped shadow on the figure to place the figure realistically in the setting.

“Barren”

    2026

  • Photoshop / Digital Collage

This photograph is a composite of two images: an empty field and a dilapidated waterworks building. In order to merge the two photos, I adjusted the exposure and color balance of the building. I faced the challenge of the building being photographed in a different light than the field, so I utilized drop shadow and area selection to make the building realistically placed in the field.

“Memories”

    2026

  • Photoshop / Digital Collage / Shadows

This photograph is a composite of two image aspects: an arched tree and playground swing. I used layer masks and blending to merge the two photos harmoniously. I adjusted the color balance and lighting to seamlessly place the swing onto the tree. This piece was designed to be reminiscent of childhood tree swings.

“Happy Birthday”

    2026

  • Digital Animation / Adobe Animate / Adobe Illustrator

This piece is a greeting card animation in the theme of Winnie the Pooh. I utilized Adobe Illustrator and Animate to create this piece. I experimented with movie clips and tweening to get the smooth flow to the animation.

“Fairy's Flight”

    2025

  • Digital Animation / Adobe Animate / Adobe Illustrator

This piece is a locomotion animation. I utilized both Adobe Illustrator and Animate to create this piece. I experimented with movie clips and frame-by-frame animation. I faced challenges with adding movement within features in the animation, which I overcame by utilizing graphic movie clips and adding color effects.

Traditional Art Projects

“Untitled”

    2024

  • Charcoal and White Chalk on Paper

This piece is a work from direct observation. It is an exploration of defining forms of various dimensions, lighting, and tonal shades.

“Practice Makes Purpose”

    2024

  • Graphite on Vellum and Paper

This piece is a different take on the cliche “Practice makes perfect”. The work represents progression, and how, with time and practice, meaning and purpose are achieved, rather than perfection. I explored with frame-by-frame storytelling, allowing the viewer to flip through the piece and see where the dancer is at the beginning and end of their dance.

“The Bounty”

    2025

  • 18x24 / Oil on Canvas

This piece was part of a series exploring small objects and reimagining them on a larger scale. I was inspired by Orazio Gentileschi’s David and Goliath for the composition of this piece. I experimented and used chiaroscuro and glazing to add a more dramatic effect to the texture and values in the piece. The figures and background scene were painted from observation.

"Sitting in Tranquility"

    2025

  • 18x24 / Oil on Canvas

This piece is part of a series exploring small objects and reimagining them on a larger scale. I used blending and layering to get the effect of rippled water disturbed by the objects sitting barely within. The objects and background were painted from observation.

“KRE”

    2025

  • Sharpie on Paper

This piece was exploring ASL. I was inspired by the composition of zentangles and created my own zentangle designs to compose the background. Precise linework was used to make the hands, which altogether spell out “KRE”, my initials.

“He's a Pirate”

    2023

  • Acrylic on Canvas

This piece was inspired by the color scheme of Pop Art paintings. By utilizing bright and vibrant colors on what would be a neutral figure, I executed an effect of the figure popping off the canvas. In order to bring the small figure to a larger scale, I was required to solve the technical challenge of utilizing a grid system.

“Smile”

    2023

  • Oil Pastel on Bristol

This piece explored the theme of arbitrary color and portraiture. I used a blending and sgraffito to achieve the smooth appearance of the skin and textured quality of the hair and background, which required me to face the challenge of oil pastels smudging unintentionally with other colors.

“On the Outside”

    2025

  • 16x12 / Oil on Wood

This piece was an exploration in self-portraiture. I used color mixing and blending to achieve the highlights and shadows of the human face, making it appear more realistic. This piece was painted from direct observation, which led to the technical challenge of painting oneself over a long period of time from a mirror view.

“The Mask”

    2025

  • 15x20 / Sharpie, Graphite, and Paper on Bristol

This piece explored mixing drawing with collage. I experimented with frottage and added sections of found textures to the piece through collage. I also experimented with numerous designs of linework in order to convey multiple textures, to complement the textures being introduced with the frottage.

“Musicality”

    2023

  • Oil on Bristol

This piece was work in portraiture. The splattering technique was used to add a celestial texture to the background. Thin linework was utilized to achieve the detailing in the strands of hair. This work represents the impact of music, how music can transport the listener

“Patriotic Comfort”

    2025

  • 12x15 Ink Print Collage

This piece explores the theme of American Patriotism. In honor of the 250th anniversary of the United States, this work was made to convey the passion Americans have for the place they call home. Ink printing on colored collage paper was used to achieve the three-dimensional effect of the objects in the piece.

“The Eye”

    2025

  • Ink Print

This piece experimented with copper plate printing. Utilizing engraving, I achieved very delicate linework and crosshatching to add detailing to my piece, which had me face the challenge of varying line weight and direction to convey forms and shapes.
